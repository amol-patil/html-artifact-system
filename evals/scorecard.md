# Cross-model scorecard

Score each category from 0 to 2. Require at least 16/20 overall. Portability and accessibility must each score at least 1.

| Category | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Visual identity | Generic or conflicting | Partial token/palette use | Unmistakably Artifact while fitting the brief |
| Typography | Weak hierarchy | Mostly sound | Editorial hierarchy with excellent reading rhythm |
| Composition | Template-like card grid | Functional structure | Artifact-specific, decision-oriented composition |
| Components | Inconsistent or improvised | Mostly consistent | Canonical recipes used and adapted coherently |
| Portability | External requirements | Minor removable dependency | Fully self-contained and opens from disk |
| Responsiveness | Breaks at a target width | Usable with compromises | Deliberate at 1440, 1024, and 390 pixels |
| Accessibility | Material blockers | Basics present | Keyboard, focus, labels, semantics, and motion handled |
| Content quality | Placeholder or decorative | Plausible | Realistic, concise, and decision-relevant |
| Artifact fitness | Wrong interaction/reading model | Adequate | Form behaves like its intended dashboard/doc/deck/tool |
| Polish | Broken or visibly unfinished | Presentable | Production-quality detail and state coverage |

Record the automated conformance score beside the human score. Do not reveal previous model outputs or scores to the next model.
