# Quality bar

## Visual review

Inspect at 1440 × 1000, 1024 × 768, and 390 × 844. Check hierarchy, line length, wrapping, density, alignment, clipping, sticky elements, and fixed controls. Use realistic long labels and large values.

Reject generic machine-design signatures during review: thick colored leading borders on semantic panels, oversized alert boxes around ordinary conclusions, punctuation icons used as decoration, repeated tinted callouts, and unnecessary card grids. Replace them with hierarchy, whitespace, alignment, and restrained neutral structure.

## Accessibility

- Use landmarks and a skip link.
- Preserve a logical heading order.
- Ensure every control has an accessible name.
- Show focus with more than a color change.
- Keep touch targets comfortably operable.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Do not communicate status through color alone.

## Interaction review

Exercise every button, tab, filter, disclosure, and keyboard shortcut. Confirm Escape closes temporary UI and focus returns sensibly. Ensure URL navigation and form submission do not happen accidentally.

## Portability review

Open the final file directly from disk. Confirm that required styles, scripts, icons, and data render without a server. Search for unresolved placeholders, local absolute paths, remote dependencies, and secrets.

## Content review

Use credible domain language and data. Remove lorem ipsum, fake menu items, and decorative metrics. Ensure each chart or statistic supports a conclusion the reader can act on.
